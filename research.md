My research interests include computational astrophysics, orbital dynamics, and planetary defense. I am currently working with Professor  <a href="https://www.astro.umd.edu/~dcr/">Derek Richardson</a> on studying the dynamics of the binary asteroid 65803 Didymos, the target of NASA's <a href="https://www.nasa.gov/planetarydefense/dart">Double Asteroid Redirection Test</a> (DART) mission. DART is a planetary defense mission that will test a kinetic impactor's ability to change the orbit of an asteroid by crashing into Didymos' moon at velocity of ~6km/s. I use <code>pkdgrav</code>, a parallel N-body tree code, to study the dynamical state of the Didymos system. <code>pkdgrav</code> treats each body as an aggregate of spheres to represent a 'rubble-pile', and can calculate collision and contact forces between the particles which allows us to do all kinds of fun stuff.
